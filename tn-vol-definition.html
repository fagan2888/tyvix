Measuring Realized Volatility

The "realized volatility" is defined as the annualized standard deviation of historical returns of a certain asset, so it's called "realized". Take the SPY ETF as an example. 

First we have a price series of SPY. 

Then we have a series of daily returns of SPY.

The annualized standard deviation is defined as the realized volatility of SPY.

Understanding Realized Volatility

Realized volatility measures how the returns are interspersed around the mean return. A larger realized volatility means that we have a return which is far from the mean return more frequently in the past history.
